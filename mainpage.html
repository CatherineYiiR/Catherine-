About Pedigree Dogs

Read it before getting your pedigree dogs

A pedigree dog is born from two dogs of the same breed, and is registered with a recognized club or society that maintain a register for dogs of that description. There are a number of pedigree dog registration schemes, of which the Kennel Club (KC) is the most well known. However, even though all of our dogs are beautiful pedigree dogs, you must be aware of potential health issues of the breeds before you decided to get them.

Since pedigree dogs are bred to look certain ways, their genes have been changed dramatically in the past century, which lead many of them to suffer from serious health issues. Many inherited diseases occur in the later lives of dogs, which means although the pups’ parents are healthy when the pups are born, they could still have inherited health problems due to the pedigree genes. Different breeds are having different health issues. You should read potential diseases listed for each breed carefully before contacting with the breeders.

When you purchase a dog you have a ‘duty of care’ under the Animal Welfare Act (2006). You must ensure that you meet the following needs that your dog has:



Its need for a suitable environment

Its need for a suitable diet

Its need to be able to exhibit normal behaviour

Any need it has to be housed with, or apart from, other animals

Its need to be protected from pain, suffering and disease
